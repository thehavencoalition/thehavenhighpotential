# The Haven Co: High Society Story Script Starter

## Core Premise

The player inherits a rough little storefront and turns it into **The Haven Co**, a cannabis shop, grow room, social lounge, snack bar, and entertainment spot. The business succeeds by growing product, helping customers, building community trust, and making the building feel alive.

The story should feel colorful, funny, and slightly chaotic. It is about becoming a neighborhood institution, not a crime simulator.

## Opening Scene

**Location:** Empty Haven storefront.

**Mascot:** Welcome to The Haven, boss. Is it glamorous? No. Is the floor sticky? Legally, I cannot answer that. But this place has potential.

**Player:** This is smaller than I expected.

**Mascot:** Small is just big before the money shows up. First job: get the grow side running. No plants, no product. No product, no customers. No customers, no empire.

## Style Closet Progression

The owner begins with starter looks, then earns stronger cosmetics from story beats. The mascot treats every unlock like brand strategy, while NPCs use the outfits as little character moments.

**Unlocks:**

- VIP Velvet from Velma after Build the Vibe.
- Comedy Jacket from Rico after the first event.
- Paperwork Blazer from Jules after unlocking Security.

- Permit Pin after handling a Secret Shopper.

## Seed Vault Story Layer

The Seed Vault starts with Blue Dream, OG Kush, and Sour Diesel. More seed names unlock through level, reputation, story chapters, events, and upgrade-gate quests. The mascot treats new seeds like collectible business opportunities, while employees argue over which ones deserve shelf space.

**Mascot:** Boss, the Seed Vault is not just a menu. It is a tiny museum where every exhibit can pay rent.

**Mira:** Faster strains are great until the lights start sweating. Upgrade the grow side before you promise miracles.

**Sav:** I do not care what the label says if the counter is empty. Grow it, stock it, then brag.

**Nova:** Anything with dessert energy gets priority. That is not a policy. Yet.

Seed stats in the prototype are fictional gameplay stats, not real-world plant or effect claims.

## Upgrade Gate Structure

Every fifth upgrade creates a story beat. The NPC is not just flavor. They become part of the world and can later appear in quests, warnings, events, or expansion scenes.

### Grow Lights Level 5: Bright Ideas

**NPC:** Mira Watts

**Scene Setup:** The grow lights flicker, the fan slows, and the mascot looks nervous.

**Mascot:** Boss, good news: the plants love the new lights. Bad news: the wall made a sound walls should not make.

**Mascot:** That is the official sound of progress.

**Mira:** You are running grow lights, a register, snack coolers, and stage dreams through one tired breaker. I can fix it, but I need power modules and proof this place can stay busy.

## Act Direction

### Act 1: Open the Doors

The player learns planting, harvesting, stocking, and serving.

### Act 2: Build the Vibe

The lounge unlocks. Customers stop being only buyers and start becoming regulars.

### Act 3: Munchies Make Money

The snack bar turns customer downtime into extra income and comedy. Once Snack Bar Level 1 is unlocked, the player gets a Snack Rush action that pulls customers toward the bar, sells snacks directly, and restocks the shelf when supplies run low.

### Act 4: Bring Entertainment In

The stage unlocks. Events create bigger crowds, bigger rewards, and more Heat.

### Act 5: Heat on the Block

The city starts watching. Security, paperwork, and community reputation matter.

Legal pressure should mostly feel like a warning light, not a constant punishment. At yellow or red Heat, an occasional Secret Shopper can appear. They are subtle, funny, and a little dramatic.

**Secret Shopper Sample Line:**

**Secret Shopper:** Lovely counter. Hypothetically, where would one find your noise policy, current inventory log, and strongest mint?

**Mascot:** Boss, that customer has clipboard energy. Nobody panic in a way that looks like panic.

### Act 6: Rival Pressure

Cloud Nine Collective starts competing for customers and attention.

### Act 7: High Society

The Haven becomes a known lifestyle brand with VIP events, signature strains, and expansion pressure.

### Daily Loop: Keep The Haven Moving

After the current story chapters, the quest panel rolls into repeatable daily goals. These loops ask the player to harvest, stock the counter, sell the strain mix, run events, serve snacks, and manage Heat so the prototype can continue indefinitely while future chapters are added.
