## Shared Character Style

- Adult cartoon characters with toy-like proportions.
- Slightly oversized heads, expressive faces, clean outlines, and readable silhouettes.
- Semi-3D mobile tycoon polish with warm studio lighting, soft shadows, and saturated colors.
- Outfits should communicate gameplay roles from a distance.
- Cannabis-culture cues should stay tasteful: colors, tiny pins, shop props, leaf motifs, menus, and product-display language.
- Avoid realistic consumption animations. Keep the game focused on business, community, comedy, and entertainment.

## Main Cast

### Player Owner

The customizable founder of The Haven Co. The concept sheet shows one default style, but the game should keep avatar customization as the real player identity.

Gameplay role:

- Appears in tutorial scenes, upgrade celebrations, city hall beats, and expansion moments.
- Outfit options should communicate owner styles: streetwear founder, grow-room worker, lounge host, stage-night fit, and compliance-ready blazer.

### Haven Mascot

The brand face, tutorial guide, narrator, and comedy alarm system.

Gameplay role:

- Explains mechanics.
- Pops up for quests, warnings, and story gates.
- Should float as a badge, screen avatar, or animated sign instead of walking like a normal NPC.

### Mira Watts

Grow tech for lights, fans, power, and grow-room machinery.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Grow Lights Level 5.
- Helps unlock better grow-room automation and rare-strain setup.

### Uncle Darnell

Neighborhood grow mentor and plant-row specialist.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Plant Rows Level 5.
- Helps expand beds, irrigation, propagation, and respectful grow-room flow.

### Sav Cruz

Counter designer and checkout-flow expert.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Customer Counter Level 5.
- Helps speed lines, improve product displays, and reduce customer impatience.

### Velma Vibe

Lounge curator with a warm, dramatic design sense.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Lounge Seating Level 5.
- Helps turn Vibe into loyalty, tips, reviews, and longer customer stays.

### Nova Sweets

Snack bar strategist with bright colors and high-energy comedy.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Snack Bar Level 5.
- Improves Snack Rush, cooler upgrades, premium munchies, and event snack combos.

### Rico Reyes

Stage booker and event hype manager.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Stage Gear Level 5.
- Books open mic, comedy, karaoke, local artist nights, and High Society events.

### Jules Park

Security lead and calm compliance expert.

Gameplay role:

- Appears at Security Desk Level 5.
- Helps with warning lights, spies, inspections, crowd control, and paperwork.

### Council Member Imani

City hall ally and community reputation contact.

Gameplay role:

- Anchors political favor and expansion approval.
- Should feel composed, helpful, and a little intimidating in a friendly way.

## Customer And Town NPC Archetypes

### Casual Customer

Easygoing first-time visitor. Low complexity, average spend, short visit.

### Regular

Friendly repeat customer. Higher loyalty, leaves reviews, reacts strongly to lounge upgrades.

### VIP

Premium spender. Wants high vibe, low wait time, and rare strains.

### Influencer

Creates reputation spikes when happy, but can amplify problems when lines are bad.

### Music Fan

Appears around events. Strong stage interest and good snack conversion.

### Snack Lover

Builds Munchies quickly and turns snack upgrades into visible money.

### Impatient Customer

Tests counter speed and staff coverage. Can reduce satisfaction if ignored.

### Local Neighbor

Connects the business to community trust. Responds well to park and town square actions.

### Tourist

Higher novelty spending, lower loyalty, attracted by murals, events, and town reputation.

### Secret Shopper

Checks service quality and can trigger a positive or negative review.

### Politician Assistant

Bridges city hall and the storefront. Ties favor to customer-facing professionalism.

### Undercover Rumor Customer

The occasional spy. Should look slightly too serious, with clipboard energy and suspicious timing.

### Delivery Driver

Brings snacks, parts, and story-gate pieces from the delivery yard.

### Cleaner / Maintenance Worker

Keeps the shop readable and supports future cleanliness or morale systems.

## Animation Priorities

1. Idle breathing and head turns for all NPCs.
2. Walk loops with role-specific posture.
3. Customer state reactions: happy, impatient, curious, impressed, leaving.
4. Staff work loops: water, harvest, stock, serve, clean, check IDs, carry snacks.
5. Story cutscene poses: confident, worried, explaining, celebrating, suspicious.
